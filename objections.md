# Objection Cheat Sheet — Cold Call

Quick reference for the 6 most common pushbacks. For each one: reframe, response, follow-up.

---

## 1. "I already have a website."

**Reframe:** Having a website and having a website that works are two different things. Most local business sites aren't ranked or optimized to convert.

**Response:**
> "That's great — the question is whether Google can actually rank it and whether it's converting visitors into calls. Most local business sites aren't built for either. That's exactly what I fix."

**Follow-up:**
> "When someone Googles '[your service] in Tucson,' does your site show up on the first page?"

---

## 2. "I don't have the budget right now."

**Reframe:** This isn't an expense — it's an investment with a clear ROI. The build pays for itself in one month if they get just a few extra bookings.

**Response:**
> "I totally get it. That's actually why the math matters here — this isn't an expense, it's an investment. Five extra bookings at your rate covers the build in the first month."

**Follow-up:**
> "What would five extra bookings a month mean for your business?"

---

## 3. "I handle my own Google page."

**Reframe:** Setting it up once and maintaining it actively are completely different. Google ranks pages that are continuously managed.

**Response:**
> "That's a good start. Most business owners set it up once and leave it. Google rewards pages that are actively managed — fresh photos, review responses, updated posts. That's where I come in."

**Follow-up:**
> "When did you last post an update or respond to a review on there?"

---

## 4. "Can I think about it?"

**Reframe:** They're not saying no — they're stalling because they don't have enough information or confidence. Find the real hesitation.

**Response:**
> "Absolutely. What would help you feel confident moving forward — is it seeing examples of my work, or understanding the ROI better?"

**Follow-up:**
> "Can we grab fifteen minutes this week so I can show you specifically what I'd do for your business?"

---

## 5. "I'm not sure I need this."

**Reframe:** They don't see the gap yet. Make the opportunity concrete by connecting it to revenue they're missing.

**Response:**
> "Fair enough. Let me ask you this — if five more people a month found your business online and booked with you, would that be worth it?"

**Follow-up:**
> "What does your current online visibility look like — do you show up when people search locally?"

---

## 6. "What results can you guarantee?"

**Reframe:** No one can guarantee specific rankings. But you can de-risk it with a short trial and point to realistic outcomes.

**Response:**
> "I can't guarantee a specific number — no one honestly can. What I can tell you is that businesses I work with see more calls, more walk-ins, and better Google rankings within the first 60–90 days. And if after one month you're not seeing traction, we part ways."

**Follow-up:**
> "Would you be willing to try one month at two hundred and see what happens?"

---

## Quick Pattern

Every objection follows the same three moves:

1. **Acknowledge** — don't argue, validate what they said
2. **Reframe** — shift from cost/risk to opportunity/ROI
3. **Ask a question** — keep them talking, uncover the real hesitation
