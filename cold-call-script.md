# Cold Call Script — Website Design & Google Business Profile

---

## Pricing

| Package | Price | What's Included |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| One-Time Build | $500 – $1,000 | Custom website + Google Business Profile optimization + analytics setup |
| Monthly Retainer | $200 / mo | Ongoing GBP management, review responses, content updates, monthly report |

---

## Step 1 — Opening

> "Did I catch you at a bad time?"

**If YES:**
> "No worries at all — when's a better time to reach you?"

**If NO:**
> "Perfect. Hey, I was checking out your website — looks really solid. Quick question though — how much of your business actually comes from people finding you online versus word of mouth or repeat customers?"

---

## Step 2 — Qualify the Pain

Ask all three questions. Listen more than you talk.

> "Roughly how many phone calls or walk-ins do you get per month right now?"

> "Of those, how many actually turn into bookings or sales?"

> "What's the average value of a booking for you?"

**Note:** Get their booking value — you'll use it in the next step.

---

## Step 3 — The Math Moment

Take their booking value x 5 extra bookings per month. Say the number out loud.

> "So if we get you just five more bookings a month, that's **[booking value x 5]** in extra revenue. This pays for itself in the first month."

Then flip it:

> "How much are you losing every month by not showing up in local search?"

**Example:** Nail salon, $60 avg booking x 5 = $300/mo extra. Retainer is $200. Net gain = $100 in month one, then pure profit after.

---

## Step 4 — Introduce the Offer

> "What I do is build high-converting websites Google can actually rank, optimize your Google Business Profile so people find you locally, and set up analytics so you can see exactly what's working."

> "One-time setup fee for the build. If you want, I manage it ongoing every month — reviews, listings, content — so you stay visible."

**Don't mention price here unless they ask.** Keep focus on the outcome.

---

## Step 5 — Close to a Meeting

> "Would it make sense to grab fifteen minutes next week? I can show you exactly what you're missing."

**Stop talking. Let them respond.**

If they ask about price:

> "One-time build is five hundred to a thousand depending on what you need. Ongoing management is two hundred a month — but let me show you the opportunity first, then you can decide if it makes sense."

---

## Step 6 — Retainer Objection Handling

> "You need just four extra bookings a month for this to pay for itself. We're going to get you way more than that."

> "Most businesses spending on ads spend way more than two hundred a month and see less results."

> "Would you rather pay two hundred to keep those extra bookings coming, or let it slide and lose that income?"

---

## Step 7 — Risk Reversal

> "Do the build for five hundred. Try one month at two hundred. If you're not seeing extra customers, we part ways. But I'm confident you will be."

Final close:

> "So — should we lock in fifteen minutes next week to walk through exactly what I'd do for your business?"

---

## Objection Handling

### "I already have a website."
> "That's great — the question is whether Google can actually rank it and whether it's converting visitors into calls. Most local business sites aren't built for either. That's exactly what I fix."

Follow-up: *"When someone Googles '[your service] in Tucson,' does your site show up on the first page?"*

---

### "I don't have the budget right now."
> "I totally get it. That's actually why the math matters here — this isn't an expense, it's an investment. Five extra bookings at your rate covers the build in the first month."

Follow-up: *"What would five extra bookings a month mean for your business?"*

---

### "I handle my own Google page."
> "That's a good start. Most business owners set it up once and leave it. Google rewards pages that are actively managed — fresh photos, review responses, updated posts. That's where I come in."

Follow-up: *"When did you last post an update or respond to a review on there?"*

---

### "Can I think about it?"
> "Absolutely. What would help you feel confident moving forward — is it seeing examples of my work, or understanding the ROI better?"

Follow-up: *"Can we grab fifteen minutes this week so I can show you specifically what I'd do for your business?"*

---

### "I'm not sure I need this."
> "Fair enough. Let me ask you this — if five more people a month found your business online and booked with you, would that be worth it?"

Follow-up: *"What does your current online visibility look like — do you show up when people search locally?"*

---

### "What results can you guarantee?"
> "I can't guarantee a specific number — no one honestly can. What I can tell you is that businesses I work with see more calls, more walk-ins, and better Google rankings within the first 60–90 days. And if after one month you're not seeing traction, we part ways."

Follow-up: *"Would you be willing to try one month at two hundred and see what happens?"*

---

## Tips & Notes

- **Call between 10am–12pm or 2pm–4pm.** Business owners are most available mid-morning and mid-afternoon.
- **Lead with a compliment about their current site** — it disarms them and shows you did your homework.
- **The math moment is your strongest tool.** Make it concrete and say it out loud. Don't rush past it.
- **Always close to a 15-minute meeting, not a sale.** Selling on a cold call is hard. Getting a short meeting is easy.
- **Silence after the close is good.** Don't fill it. Let them answer.
- **Follow up within 24 hours** with a short email recapping the math and linking your portfolio.
- **Target businesses with weak GBP profiles** — few reviews, no photos, incomplete info. They're the easiest to show quick wins.
